SECTION 1 — WHAT WE ARE BUILDING
================================================================================

Siren.ng is a community emergency coordination platform for Lagos, Nigeria.

THE PROBLEM (grounded in 2025 data):
Lagos State Command and Control Centre (LSCCC) handled 12,483 emergency
incidents in 2025. LASEMA recorded 1,156 incidents resulting in 173 deaths.
The top three categories by frequency:

  1. Road Traffic (RTA/HAZARD): 2,504 accident calls + 394 road crashes +
     249 tanker incidents. Daily downed power lines, fallen poles, and wire
     hazards force single-lane traffic and cause chain accidents. This is
     the #1 category by volume and daily life impact.

  2. Fire: 2,165 fire-related calls. Lagos Fire Service recorded 1,685
     outbreaks causing 133 deaths and ₦19.72 billion in property losses.
     Electrical faults (transformers, downed wires) are a leading cause.

  3. Medical Emergencies: 2,397 cases. Most linked to road injuries or
     flooding-related illness. Alimosho and Eti-Osa LGAs top incident counts.

53% of emergency calls still go unanswered. Average response delay kills people.
There is no single coordination point connecting willing helpers with people who
need them in real time.

THE SOLUTION:
One WhatsApp number. Send a message describing what you see. Within 90 seconds
Claude AI verifies it, the nearest qualified community responder gets GPS
directions to the scene, the closest clinic is alerted, and the reporter gets a
live tracking link. No app. No account. No form. Just one WhatsApp message.

Beyond emergency response, Siren also:
- Lets families save locations (home, child's school, land, elderly parent's
  compound) and get WhatsApp alerts when incidents happen nearby — GUARDIAN MODE
- Lets daily commuters save their Home + Office route and get peak-hour
  reroute alerts when incidents block their corridor — COMMUTE SHIELD
- Runs a live community resource board on each active incident where people
  can suggest and claim physical resources (cars, ladders, food, medical kits)
- Accepts donations for victims, responders, and the platform via Paystack
- Lets community members vouch for incidents to help verify them

CORE AUDIENCE (locked):
  1. Lagos families & property owners — emotional loyalty
     "What if flood hits Timi's school while I'm in traffic?"
  2. Daily 9-5 commuters — daily habit + virality
     "That downed wire just cost me 3 hours. Why didn't I know?"

These two groups cover 80%+ of Lagos adults and share one need: predictable
control in a city that feels uncontrollable. Siren gives them that.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.1 CORE PRINCIPLES — NEVER BREAK THESE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. WhatsApp is the entry point. Web is the richer experience.
   Use the foot-in-the-door approach. Zero friction to start. Bring them to
   the website for richer actions (donating, resource board, map, impact page).

2. Zero friction for reporters. One WhatsApp message. No account, no app,
   no form. Friction at entry kills reporting.

3. AI filters before humans see anything. Responders only see VERIFIED
   incidents. The feed stays trusted.

4. Every incident closes visibly. The reporter must see the loop close.
   What happened, who helped, how fast. This is what builds trust and
   distinguishes Siren from every failed government hotline before it.

5. Government is optional. Platform works fully without LASEMA.

6. No account required for the public. Reporters, vouchers, resource
   contributors, and donors are identified by WhatsApp number or a
   short-lived session token. JWT login is only for agency operators and
   platform admin. These are two completely separate auth tracks.

7. Guardian Mode and Commute Shield are passive. Once saved, they work
   silently in the background. The user does nothing. Siren does the work.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.2 THE SEVEN INCIDENT TYPES — ONLY THESE ARE ACCEPTED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Code        What qualifies
----------- ----------------------------------------------------------------
FIRE        Structure fire, vehicle fire, gas explosion.
            NOT: trash fires, small cooking fires with no spread risk.
FLOOD       Homes or roads submerged, people trapped by rising water.
            NOT: puddles or mild waterlogging.
COLLAPSE    Full or partial building collapse, imminent collapse with people.
            NOT: cracks or cosmetic damage.
RTA         Road crash with injuries or trapped victims.
            NOT: minor fender benders with no injuries.
EXPLOSION   Gas tanker, pipeline, or industrial explosion with radius risk.
DROWNING    Person unable to self-rescue in water, boat capsize, flood rescue.
HAZARD      Live downed power lines, fallen transformer, collapsed
            infrastructure threatening life or blocking a major route.
            This is the most common daily type in Lagos (power lines, poles,
            wire snaps from rain). Triggers both Guardian and Commute Shield.

Everything outside these seven is rejected at AI verification with a message
pointing the reporter to the right resource.

SECTION 6 — HAVERSINE DISTANCE UTILITY (utils/distance.py)
================================================================================

Build this before any app. Every spatial query goes through this function.
CRITICAL: Uses HAVING not WHERE. Do not change this.

# utils/distance.py
from django.db import connection


def haversine_query(table, lat_field, lng_field, ref_lat, ref_lng,
                    radius_km, extra_filters='', extra_params=None, limit=10):
    base_params = [ref_lat, ref_lng, ref_lat]
    filter_params = extra_params or []
    params = base_params + filter_params + [radius_km]

    sql = f"""
        SELECT *,
          (6371 * acos(
            LEAST(1.0,
              cos(radians(%s)) * cos(radians({lat_field})) *
              cos(radians({lng_field}) - radians(%s)) +
              sin(radians(%s)) * sin(radians({lat_field}))
            )
          )) AS distance_km
        FROM {table}
        WHERE (1=1) {extra_filters}
        HAVING distance_km <= %s
        ORDER BY distance_km ASC
        LIMIT {limit}
    """

    with connection.cursor() as cursor:
        cursor.execute(sql, params)
        columns = [col[0] for col in cursor.description]
        return [dict(zip(columns, row)) for row in cursor.fetchall()]


def point_to_line_distance(point_lat, point_lng, a_lat, a_lng, b_lat, b_lng):
    """
    Returns approximate distance in km from a point to the line segment A-B.
    Used by Commute Shield to check if an incident is on a commuter's route.
    This is a flat-earth approximation — accurate enough for Lagos corridor checks.
    """
    import math

    def to_rad(d): return d * math.pi / 180
    R = 6371.0

    # Convert to approximate x/y in km
    lat0 = (a_lat + b_lat) / 2
    dx = math.cos(to_rad(lat0))

    ax, ay = a_lng * dx, a_lat
    bx, by = b_lng * dx, b_lat
    px, py = point_lng * dx, point_lat

    # Vector AB
    abx, aby = bx - ax, by - ay
    ab_len_sq = abx * abx + aby * aby

    if ab_len_sq == 0:
        # A and B are same point
        return math.sqrt((px - ax) ** 2 + (py - ay) ** 2) * 111.0

    # Project P onto AB
    t = max(0, min(1, ((px - ax) * abx + (py - ay) * aby) / ab_len_sq))
    closest_x = ax + t * abx
    closest_y = ay + t * aby

    dist_deg = math.sqrt((px - closest_x) ** 2 + (py - closest_y) ** 2)
    return dist_deg * 111.0  # degrees to km (rough)

import uuid
from django.db import models

LOCATION_TYPES = [
    ('HOME',   'Home'),
    ('SCHOOL', 'School/Child location'),
    ('LAND',   'Land/Property'),
    ('OFFICE', 'Office/Workplace'),
    ('FAMILY', 'Family member location'),
    ('OTHER',  'Other'),
]


class LocationSubscription(models.Model):
    """
    A saved location (or commute corridor) the subscriber wants monitored.

    POINT:   Single location. Alert when incident within alert_radius_km.
    COMMUTE: Home + Office. Alert when incident within 1.5km of route
             corridor during peak hours (6-10am, 4-8pm Lagos time).
    """
    SUBSCRIPTION_TYPES = [
        ('POINT',   'Single Point — Guardian Mode'),
        ('COMMUTE', 'Commute Shield — Home + Office corridor'),
    ]

    id               = models.UUIDField(primary_key=True, default=uuid.uuid4, editable=False)
    phone_hash       = models.CharField(max_length=64)
    whatsapp_number  = models.CharField(max_length=20)
    label            = models.CharField(max_length=200)

    subscription_type = models.CharField(max_length=10, choices=SUBSCRIPTION_TYPES,
                          default='POINT')
    location_type    = models.CharField(max_length=10, choices=LOCATION_TYPES, default='HOME')

    # Home or single-point location
    location_lat     = models.FloatField()
    location_lng     = models.FloatField()

    # COMMUTE only — second pin (office)
    office_lat       = models.FloatField(null=True, blank=True)
    office_lng       = models.FloatField(null=True, blank=True)

    # Guardian Mode — list of family/property labels saved under this subscriber
    # e.g. ["Timi (school)", "Mama (Ikeja)", "Shop (Alimosho)"]
    family_members   = models.JSONField(default=list)

    alert_radius_km  = models.FloatField(default=1.0)   # for POINT subscriptions
    commute_buffer_km = models.FloatField(default=1.5)  # for COMMUTE corridor checks
    peak_only        = models.BooleanField(default=False) # auto True for COMMUTE

    is_active        = models.BooleanField(default=True)
    incident_types   = models.JSONField(default=list)   # empty = all types

    # Safety Score: 0-100. Updated daily at 6am by celery-beat task.
    # Based on incident frequency near this location in the past 30 days.
    safety_score     = models.IntegerField(default=85)

    created_at       = models.DateTimeField(auto_now_add=True)
    updated_at       = models.DateTimeField(auto_now=True)

    class Meta:
        ordering = ['label']

import json, re
from celery import shared_task
from django.conf import settings
import anthropic


@shared_task(bind=True, max_retries=3, default_retry_delay=10)
def verify_incident_ai(self, incident_id: str):
    from apps.incidents.models import Incident

    incident = Incident.objects.get(id=incident_id)

    # v5: Extended prompt to detect infrastructure hazards
    prompt = f"""You are an emergency verification system for Lagos, Nigeria.
Analyse this report and respond ONLY with valid JSON. No markdown. No explanation.

Report: {incident.description}
Location text: {incident.address_text}

Return this exact JSON:
{{
  "eligible": true or false,
  "rejection_reason": "why rejected, or null if eligible",
  "incident_type": "FIRE|FLOOD|COLLAPSE|RTA|EXPLOSION|DROWNING|HAZARD or null",
  "severity": "LOW|MEDIUM|HIGH|CRITICAL",
  "ai_confidence": 0.0 to 1.0,
  "fraud_score": 0.0 to 1.0,
  "is_infrastructure": true or false,
  "suggested_skills": ["list of skill codes"],
  "reasoning": "one sentence"
}}

Only these types qualify: FIRE FLOOD COLLAPSE RTA EXPLOSION DROWNING HAZARD
fraud_score above 0.7 means the report looks fake.
is_infrastructure must be true if the report mentions transformer, wire, pole,
power line, NEPA, EKEDC, fallen cable, or electrical infrastructure of any kind."""

    try:
        client = anthropic.Anthropic(api_key=settings.ANTHROPIC_API_KEY)
        message = client.messages.create(
            model=settings.ANTHROPIC_MODEL,
            max_tokens=1024,
            messages=[{'role': 'user', 'content': prompt}]
        )
        text = message.content[0].text.strip()
        text = re.sub(r'```json|```', '', text).strip()
        result = json.loads(text)
    except Exception as exc:
        raise self.retry(exc=exc)

    incident.ai_raw_response   = result
    incident.ai_confidence     = result.get('ai_confidence', 0.0)
    incident.fraud_score       = result.get('fraud_score', 0.0)
    incident.is_infrastructure = result.get('is_infrastructure', False)

    if not result.get('eligible') or incident.fraud_score > 0.7:
        _transition(incident, 'REJECTED', 'AI', result.get('rejection_reason', ''))
        incident.save()
        _notify_rejected(incident, result.get('rejection_reason', ''))
        return

    incident.incident_type = result.get('incident_type', '')
    incident.severity      = result.get('severity', 'MEDIUM')
    incident.set_vouch_threshold()

    if incident.ai_confidence >= 0.75:
        _transition(incident, 'VERIFIED', 'AI',
                    f'Auto-verified. Confidence: {incident.ai_confidence:.2f}')
        incident.save()
        _post_verification_actions(incident)
    elif incident.ai_confidence >= 0.4:
        _transition(incident, 'VERIFYING', 'AI', 'Awaiting community vouches')
        incident.save()
        _notify_verifying(incident)
    else:
        _transition(incident, 'REJECTED', 'AI', 'Insufficient confidence')
        incident.save()


def _transition(incident, new_status, actor, note=''):
    from apps.incidents.models import ResponseLog
    from apps.incidents.consumers import broadcast_update
    ResponseLog.objects.create(
        incident=incident, from_status=incident.status,
        to_status=new_status, actor=actor, note=note
    )
    incident.status = new_status
    broadcast_update(incident)


def _post_verification_actions(incident):
    from apps.whatsapp.tasks import notify_reporter_verified, post_community_announcement
    from apps.responders.tasks import notify_nearest_responders
    from apps.organisations.tasks import notify_nearest_organisations
    from apps.subscriptions.tasks import (
        notify_location_subscribers, notify_commute_shield
    )

    notify_reporter_verified.delay(str(incident.id))
    post_community_announcement.delay(str(incident.id))

    if incident.severity in ['CRITICAL', 'HIGH', 'MEDIUM']:
        notify_nearest_responders.delay(str(incident.id))

    notify_nearest_organisations.delay(str(incident.id))
    notify_location_subscribers.delay(str(incident.id))

    # v5: Commute Shield — runs for all road/infrastructure/flood incidents
    if incident.is_infrastructure or incident.incident_type in ['HAZARD', 'RTA', 'FLOOD']:
        notify_commute_shield.delay(str(incident.id))

def route_inbound(from_number, body, media_urls, location):
    body_upper = body.strip().upper()

    if body_upper in ['YES', 'NO', 'ONSCENE', 'DONE', 'HELP']:
        return handle_responder_reply(from_number, body_upper)

    if body_upper in ['ACCEPT', 'DECLINE', 'CALL']:
        return handle_org_reply(from_number, body_upper)

    if body_upper == 'RESPONDER':
        return start_responder_registration(from_number)
    if body_upper == 'REGISTER ORG':
        return start_org_registration(from_number)

    if body_upper == 'WATCH':
        return start_subscription_flow(from_number)
    if body_upper == 'MY ALERTS':
        return show_subscriptions(from_number)
    if body_upper.startswith('STOP'):
        return handle_stop_command(from_number, body)
    if body_upper.startswith('VOUCH'):
        return handle_vouch_command(from_number, body)

    # v5 new commands
    if body_upper in ['POINT', 'COMMUTE']:
        return handle_subscription_type_choice(from_number, body_upper)
    if body_upper == 'MY COMMUTE':
        return show_commute_briefing(from_number)
    if body_upper == 'MY IMPACT':
        return send_impact_link(from_number)
    if body_upper == 'NEED RIDE':
        return handle_need_ride(from_number)
    if body_upper.startswith('BRING'):
        return handle_bring_command(from_number, body)

    if location and location.get('latitude'):
        return handle_location_share(from_number, location, body)

    if is_onboarding(from_number):
        return handle_onboarding_step(from_number, body)

    return create_incident_from_message(from_number, body, media_urls, location)

@shared_task
def notify_location_subscribers(incident_id: str):
    """
    Find all active POINT subscriptions near the incident.
    Alert subscribers. Record SubscriptionAlert to prevent duplicates.
    """
    from apps.incidents.models import Incident
    from apps.subscriptions.models import LocationSubscription, SubscriptionAlert
    from apps.whatsapp.tasks import send_whatsapp_text
    from utils.distance import haversine_query

    incident = Incident.objects.get(id=incident_id)
    if not incident.location_lat or not incident.location_lng:
        return

    nearby = haversine_query(
        table='subscriptions_locationsubscription',
        lat_field='location_lat', lng_field='location_lng',
        ref_lat=incident.location_lat, ref_lng=incident.location_lng,
        radius_km=10.0,
        extra_filters="AND is_active = TRUE AND subscription_type = 'POINT'",
        limit=500
    )

    for sub_data in nearby:
        if sub_data['distance_km'] > sub_data['alert_radius_km']:
            continue

        types_filter = sub_data.get('incident_types', [])
        if types_filter and incident.incident_type not in types_filter:
            continue

        sub = LocationSubscription.objects.get(id=sub_data['id'])

        if SubscriptionAlert.objects.filter(subscription=sub, incident=incident).exists():
            continue

        message = _build_guardian_alert(incident, sub_data)
        send_whatsapp_text.delay(sub_data['whatsapp_number'], message)

        SubscriptionAlert.objects.create(
            subscription=sub, incident=incident,
            distance_km=sub_data['distance_km'], alert_type='POINT'
        )


def _build_guardian_alert(incident, sub_data):
    type_labels = {
        'FIRE':'Fire Outbreak', 'FLOOD':'Flooding', 'COLLAPSE':'Building Collapse',
        'RTA':'Road Accident', 'EXPLOSION':'Explosion',
        'DROWNING':'Drowning', 'HAZARD':'Hazard/Infrastructure'
    }
    return (
        f"SIREN ALERT — {sub_data['label']}\n\n"
        f"{type_labels.get(incident.incident_type, incident.incident_type)}\n"
        f"Severity: {incident.severity}\n"
        f"Location: {incident.address_text or incident.zone_name}\n"
        f"Distance from {sub_data['label']}: {sub_data['distance_km']:.1f}km\n\n"
        f"Full details and support:\n"
        f"siren.ng/track/{incident.id}\n\n"
        f"Reply STOP {sub_data['label']} to pause alerts for this location."
    )

@shared_task
def notify_commute_shield(incident_id: str):
    """
    Find all active COMMUTE subscriptions whose home-office corridor passes
    within commute_buffer_km of this incident.
    Only runs during peak hours Lagos time (6-10am, 4-8pm).
    """
    from apps.incidents.models import Incident
    from apps.subscriptions.models import LocationSubscription, SubscriptionAlert
    from apps.whatsapp.tasks import send_whatsapp_text
    from utils.distance import point_to_line_distance
    from django.utils import timezone
    import pytz

    incident = Incident.objects.get(id=incident_id)
    if not incident.location_lat or not incident.location_lng:
        return

    # Peak-hour check
    lagos_tz = pytz.timezone('Africa/Lagos')
    now_lagos = timezone.now().astimezone(lagos_tz)
    hour = now_lagos.hour
    is_peak = (6 <= hour < 10) or (16 <= hour < 20)
    # Still run for CRITICAL severity outside peak hours
    if not is_peak and incident.severity != 'CRITICAL':
        return

    # Fetch all active COMMUTE subscriptions that have office coordinates
    commute_subs = LocationSubscription.objects.filter(
        subscription_type='COMMUTE',
        is_active=True,
        office_lat__isnull=False,
        office_lng__isnull=False
    )

    for sub in commute_subs:
        # Check if incident is within the commute corridor buffer
        dist = point_to_line_distance(
            incident.location_lat, incident.location_lng,
            sub.location_lat, sub.location_lng,   # home
            sub.office_lat, sub.office_lng          # office
        )

        if dist > sub.commute_buffer_km:
            continue

        if SubscriptionAlert.objects.filter(subscription=sub, incident=incident).exists():
            continue

        message = _build_commute_alert(incident, sub, dist)
        send_whatsapp_text.delay(sub.whatsapp_number, message)

        SubscriptionAlert.objects.create(
            subscription=sub, incident=incident,
            distance_km=dist, alert_type='COMMUTE'
        )


def _build_commute_alert(incident, sub, distance_km):
    type_labels = {
        'RTA': 'Road Accident', 'HAZARD': 'Downed wire / road hazard',
        'FLOOD': 'Flooding', 'FIRE': 'Fire', 'EXPLOSION': 'Explosion',
        'COLLAPSE': 'Building Collapse', 'DROWNING': 'Drowning'
    }
    return (
        f"COMMUTE SHIELD — {type_labels.get(incident.incident_type, incident.incident_type)}"
        f" on your route\n\n"
        f"{incident.address_text or incident.zone_name}\n"
        f"Severity: {incident.severity}\n"
        f"Distance from your corridor: {distance_km:.1f}km\n\n"
        f"Reply NEED RIDE to connect with people offering transport.\n\n"
        f"Full update: siren.ng/track/{incident.id}"
    )

@shared_task
def daily_safety_score_update():
    """
    For every active subscription, count verified incidents in the past 30 days
    within the alert radius (POINT) or corridor (COMMUTE).
    Score formula: starts at 100, -5 per incident, floor at 0.
    Save score and log entry.
    """
    from apps.subscriptions.models import LocationSubscription, SafetyScoreLog
    from apps.incidents.models import Incident
    from utils.distance import haversine_query
    from django.utils import timezone
    from datetime import timedelta

    cutoff = timezone.now() - timedelta(days=30)

    for sub in LocationSubscription.objects.filter(is_active=True):
        # Count incidents near this location in last 30 days
        nearby = haversine_query(
            table='incidents_incident',
            lat_field='location_lat', lng_field='location_lng',
            ref_lat=sub.location_lat, ref_lng=sub.location_lng,
            radius_km=sub.alert_radius_km,
            extra_filters="AND status IN ('VERIFIED','RESPONDING','AGENCY_NOTIFIED','RESOLVED')",
            limit=100
        )
        # Filter by date in Python (haversine doesn't take datetime params easily)
        recent = [r for r in nearby
                  if r.get('created_at') and r['created_at'] >= cutoff]

        score = max(0, 100 - (len(recent) * 5))
        reason = f"{len(recent)} verified incident(s) within {sub.alert_radius_km}km in last 30 days."

        sub.safety_score = score
        sub.save(update_fields=['safety_score'])

        SafetyScoreLog.objects.create(subscription=sub, score=score, reason=reason)

@shared_task
def send_commute_briefing():
    """
    Send daily route briefing to all active COMMUTE subscribers.
    Checks for any incidents on their corridor in the last 24 hours.
    """
    from apps.subscriptions.models import LocationSubscription
    from apps.incidents.models import Incident
    from apps.whatsapp.tasks import send_whatsapp_text
    from utils.distance import point_to_line_distance
    from django.utils import timezone
    from datetime import timedelta
    import pytz

    cutoff = timezone.now() - timedelta(hours=24)
    lagos_tz = pytz.timezone('Africa/Lagos')
    hour = timezone.now().astimezone(lagos_tz).hour
    is_morning = hour < 12

    for sub in LocationSubscription.objects.filter(
        subscription_type='COMMUTE', is_active=True,
        office_lat__isnull=False, office_lng__isnull=False
    ):
        # Check for recent incidents on corridor
        recent_incidents = Incident.objects.filter(
            status__in=['VERIFIED','RESPONDING','AGENCY_NOTIFIED','RESOLVED'],
            created_at__gte=cutoff,
            incident_type__in=['HAZARD','RTA','FLOOD']
        ).exclude(location_lat=None)

        corridor_incidents = []
        for inc in recent_incidents:
            dist = point_to_line_distance(
                inc.location_lat, inc.location_lng,
                sub.location_lat, sub.location_lng,
                sub.office_lat, sub.office_lng
            )
            if dist <= sub.commute_buffer_km:
                corridor_incidents.append((inc, dist))

        message = _build_briefing_message(sub, corridor_incidents, is_morning)
        send_whatsapp_text.delay(sub.whatsapp_number, message)


def _build_briefing_message(sub, incidents, is_morning):
    greeting = "Good morning" if is_morning else "Evening check"
    direction = f"{sub.label} route"

    if not incidents:
        return (
            f"{greeting}. Your {direction}:\n\n"
            f"Clear — no incidents on your corridor in the last 24 hours.\n"
            f"Safety Score: {sub.safety_score}\n\n"
            f"Stay safe out there."
        )

    inc, dist = incidents[0]  # Most relevant
    return (
        f"{greeting}. Your {direction}:\n\n"
        f"{inc.incident_type} reported {dist:.1f}km from your corridor.\n"
        f"{inc.address_text or inc.zone_name}\n"
        f"Status: {inc.status}\n\n"
        f"Safety Score: {sub.safety_score}\n\n"
        f"Full map: siren.ng/map"
    )

12.1 HOW PAYSTACK SPLITS WORK

Siren uses Paystack sub-accounts to automatically split at transaction level.

Fund logic:
  PLATFORM:  100% to Siren main account.
  RESPONDER: Siren takes 10%. 90% split equally among responders with
             ONSCENE status for this incident. If no responders on scene,
             held in Siren account until they arrive.
  VICTIM:    Siren takes 10%. 90% to Victim Relief holding sub-account.
             Released when reporter provides recipient bank details.
             If no recipient after 48 hours: donor WhatsApp asks to redirect.

12.2 PAYSTACK INTEGRATION FLOW

  Step 1: POST /api/resources/donate/
          Body: {incident_id, amount_naira, fund_choice, donor_email}
          Backend creates Donation (PENDING), calls Paystack initialize,
          returns {payment_url}

  Step 2: Frontend redirects to payment_url (Paystack hosted page)

  Step 3: Donor completes payment

  Step 4: POST /api/resources/donate/verify/ (Paystack webhook)
          Validate X-Paystack-Signature (HMAC SHA512 with PAYSTACK_SECRET_KEY)
          On success: update Donation, update Incident totals, trigger split

  Step 5: Frontend /donate/success?ref={ref}
          Fetches updated donation summary

SECTION 17 — CRITICAL NOTES FOR CLAUDE CODE
================================================================================

Read all of these before writing a single line of code.

1.  NO GEODJANGO EVER.
    Every models.py: from django.db import models
    NEVER: from django.contrib.gis.db import models
    NEVER: PointField, PolygonField, GEOSGeometry, any gis import.
    All location = two FloatFields. All spatial = utils/distance.py.

2.  HAVERSINE USES HAVING NOT WHERE.
    distance_km is a computed column alias. HAVING filters it.
    Do not change HAVING to WHERE. It will break. Do not "fix" it.

3.  TWILIO SIGNATURE VALIDATION IS MANDATORY.
    from twilio.request_validator import RequestValidator
    validator = RequestValidator(settings.TWILIO_AUTH_TOKEN)
    Reject (403) if validation fails.

4.  WHATSAPP NUMBER FORMAT.
    Always: 'whatsapp:+2348012345678'
    Strip prefix when storing: phone = from_number.replace('whatsapp:', '')

5.  CLAUDE RESPONSE CLEANING.
    text = re.sub(r'```json|```', '', text).strip()
    Then: result = json.loads(text)

6.  PHONE PRIVACY.
    Store SHA256(phone) as phone_hash. Keep actual number only where needed
    to send messages. import hashlib.
    hashlib.sha256(phone.encode()).hexdigest()

7.  JWT ONLY FOR AGENCY/ADMIN DASHBOARD.
    Public users never log in. Identified by phone_hash or session token.
    No conflict with public no-account approach.

8.  JWT IN REACT: useState or Context only. Never localStorage.

9.  LEAFLET CSS: first import in main.tsx. Non-negotiable.

10. CELERY DEVELOPMENT: CELERY_ALWAYS_EAGER=True. Tasks run synchronously.
    Set False in production.

11. SUPABASE: Direct Connection URI only. Never the pooler string.

12. PAYSTACK WEBHOOK VALIDATION.
    import hmac, hashlib
    sig = hmac.new(secret.encode(), body_bytes, hashlib.sha512).hexdigest()
    Reject if signature does not match X-Paystack-Signature header.

13. DONATION AMOUNTS IN KOBO.
    Store in kobo. Display in naira. amount_kobo = int(amount_naira * 100)

14. SUBSCRIPTION BROAD SWEEP.
    notify_location_subscribers uses 10km broad sweep, then filters per
    subscription's own alert_radius_km in Python. Do not change the 10km.

15. COMMUTE SHIELD PEAK HOURS.
    Lagos time (Africa/Lagos). Morning: 6-10am. Evening: 4-8pm (16-20).
    Always run for CRITICAL severity regardless of time.

16. POINT-TO-LINE DISTANCE IS APPROXIMATE.
    point_to_line_distance uses flat-earth approximation. Accurate enough
    for Lagos corridor checks (max 50km). Do not replace with exact geodesic.

17. RESOURCECLAIM DUPLICATE.
    Catch IntegrityError on claim creation. Return 200 with
    "You have already claimed this item" — not 400.

18. SAFETY SCORE FORMULA.
    Starts at 100. Subtract 5 per verified incident within radius in 30 days.
    Floor at 0. Never go above 100. Updated daily at 6am by celery-beat.

19. COMMUTE BRIEFING RUNS TWICE DAILY.
    Same task (send_commute_briefing) runs at 6:30am and 4:30pm.
    The task detects morning/evening from the current Lagos hour.
    Do not create two separate tasks.

20. MY IMPACT PAGE IS PUBLIC.
    phone_hash is in the URL query string (?phone_hash=...).
    No authentication. The hash itself is the access token.
    Do not add JWT auth to this endpoint.
